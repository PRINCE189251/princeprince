The Bhagavad Gita, a timeless scripture from the Indian epic Mahabharata, is a dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna. On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna is torn by doubt and despair, and Krishna guides him with wisdom that transcends time.

The Gita is not merely about war‚Äîit is about life‚Äôs choices, the path of righteousness (Dharma), and the pursuit of self-realization. It blends philosophy, spirituality, and practical guidance for living with purpose and peace.

Its teachings emphasize balance between action and detachment, devotion and knowledge, reminding us that true freedom comes from fulfilling our duties with a steady mind and without attachment to outcomes.

Key Lessons

  • Perform your duties selflessly without attachment to results (Karma Yoga).
  • Balance action, devotion, and knowledge as paths to self-realization.
  • Control desires and emotions through discipline and self-mastery.
  • See the divine presence in all beings and live with compassion.
  • True inner peace comes from surrendering to the higher self and Dharma.
